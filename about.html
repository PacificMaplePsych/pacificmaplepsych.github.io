About

Founded in 2018, Pacific Maple Psych Centre is a growing multidisciplinary mental health practice. We are also one of Vancouver's top autism assessment clinics for all ages. Our goal is to serve the various mental health, self-discovery, and personal growth needs of Canada's Pacific Northwest. Though we provide virtual care across British Columbia, our physical location is conveniently situated in the beautifully restored Rogers Heritage Building, across the street from the Waterfront Canada Line Station and a short walk from Granville SkyTrain Station, with easy parking at the back of the building.

We have compassion for people from all walks of life. Our clinicians are familiar with neurodiversity-affirming care, trauma-informed support, culturally responsive practice, and other inclusive, anti-oppressive approaches. We also recognize experiences such as vystopia (vegan outcast syndrome) and support individuals who feel marginalized or misunderstood because of their identity, values, or lifestyle.

We are the top external practicum location for students of Adler University. Though we often receive requests from practicum students at SFU and UBC, we currently do not have capacity to take all such requests. However, our practice is growing, and we hope to eventually be able to take more students. We welcome volunteers interested in community service opportunities, such as providing educational presentations about psychology in schools and the broader community.

Demand for services is often higher than capacity at Pacific Maple Psych Centre. As a result, we are often open to hiring mental health professionals, from licensed psychologists and therapists to select coaches with a talent for transforming lives. We seek the utmost excellence to ensure we are providing the highest standard of care.

We are a values-driven clinic with an emphasis on community involvement. As such, a portion of our profits are donated to select community groups and charities. Details to be added in the future.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What services does Pacific Maple Psych Centre offer?

We offer a range of services including psychological assessments, autism assessments, individual therapy, group therapy, and couples therapy.

Do you provide care in person, virtually, or both?

We provide virtual care across British Columbia, and our physical location is in downtown Vancouver in the Rogers Heritage Building, across from Waterfront Station and a short walk from Granville SkyTrain Station.

Who do you work with?

We support children, adolescents, adults, couples, and families seeking assessment, therapy, and practical guidance related to mental health, relationships, neurodevelopmental questions, and personal growth.

Are you neurodiversity-affirming and trauma-informed?

Yes. Our clinicians are familiar with neurodiversity-affirming care, trauma-informed support, culturally responsive practice, and other inclusive, anti-oppressive approaches. We aim to provide care that is respectful, compassionate, and responsive to each person’s context and needs.

Do you offer autism assessments for children and adults?

Yes. We offer Autism Spectrum Disorder assessments for children, adolescents, and adults in Vancouver and across British Columbia.

Do you offer ADHD, psychoeducational, or other psychological assessments?

Yes. Our psychological assessment services may include ADHD, psychoeducational, integrated, and other forms of assessment designed to provide diagnostic clarification, practical recommendations, and written reports.

Are your services covered by insurance?

Coverage depends on your specific plan. We recommend checking with your insurance provider regarding coverage for services offered by a registered psychologist or other eligible mental health professional in British Columbia.

Do you also offer therapy if I am not looking for an assessment?

Yes. We offer individual therapy, group therapy, and couples therapy in addition to assessment services.

How do I book an appointment?

You can reach out by phone or email, or book online through our website. Our team can help direct you to the service that best fits your needs, whether you are looking for therapy, assessment, or diagnostic clarification.

What if I am not sure which service is right for me?

That is common. If you are unsure whether you need therapy, an autism assessment, an ADHD or psychoeducational assessment, or another service, contacting the clinic is a good first step so we can help guide you toward the best fit.
