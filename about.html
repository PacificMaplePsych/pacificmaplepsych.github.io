About

Founded in 2018, Pacific Maple Psych Centre is a growing multidisciplinary mental health practice. Our goal is to serve the various mental health, self-discovery, and personal growth needs of Canada's Pacific Northwest. Though we provide virtual care across British Columbia, our physical location is conveniently situated in the beautifully restored Rogers Heritage Building, across the street from the Waterfront Canada Line Station, and a short walk from Granville SkyTrain Station.

We have compassion for people from all walks of life, with clinicians familiarized in neurodiversity-affirming care, trauma-informed support, culturally responsive practice, LGBTQ2S+ affirming care, anti-oppressive frameworks, and disability-informed approaches. We also understand experiences like vystopia (or vegan outcast syndrome), and we support any individuals struggling with discrimination, marginalization, or repeated violations of their dignity and rights.

We are the top external practicum location for students of Adler University. Though we often receive requests from practicum students at SFU and UBC, we currently do not have capacity to take all such requests. However, our practice is growing, and we hope to eventually be able to take more students. We are always open to volunteers seeking community service opportunities.

Demand for services is often higher than capacity at Pacific Maple Psych Centre. As a result, we are often open to hiring mental health professionals, from licensed psychologists and therapists to select coaches with a talent for transforming lives. We seek the utmost excellence to ensure we are providing the highest standard of care.

We are a values-driven clinic with an emphasis on community involvement. As such, a portion of our profits are donated to select community groups and charities. Details to be added in the future.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What types of services do you offer?

We provide a range of mental health services, including psychological assessment, individual therapy, group therapy, Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) assessments, and couples therapy. Our services cater to individuals of all ages.

What qualifications do your professionals possess?

Our registered psychologists and clinical counsellors are highly qualified professionals who hold advanced degrees in psychology or related fields. They have extensive training and experience in the field. For student therapy, psychology students may offer in-person and teletherapy sessions under supervision.

Are your services covered by insurance?

We recommend checking with your insurance provider regarding coverage for psychological services offered by a Registered Psychologist in British Columbia. If fees are not covered or you do not have health coverage, psychological services may qualify as a medical expense and may be tax deductible.

How can I schedule an appointment?

To schedule an appointment, you can reach out by phone or email, or book online through the Book Now page. Our team will help you find a convenient time for an initial consultation or ongoing sessions.

What can I expect during a psychological assessment?

Our evaluations may include record reviews, clinical interviews, interviews with parents, siblings, peers, and teachers, rating scales, observations, and standardized tests. This multi-faceted approach helps us provide meaningful insights and recommendations.

How long does the psychological assessment process typically take?

The psychological assessment process generally spans over the course of three days. This allows time for a clinical virtual interview and for evaluations and assessments to be completed comprehensively.
