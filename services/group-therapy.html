Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is group therapy?

Group therapy is a structured form of psychotherapy where several participants meet together with a therapist to discuss challenges, learn skills, and support one another in a guided environment.

Is group therapy effective?

Group therapy can be highly effective for concerns such as anxiety, depression, social difficulties, and stress. The group setting allows participants to learn from others and experience a sense of connection.

How many people are in each group therapy session?

Most therapy groups include a maximum of eight participants so that everyone has time to participate and receive support.

Is group therapy confidential?

Yes. Participants are expected to respect the privacy of others and keep information shared during the sessions confidential.

Do I have to talk in group therapy?

Sharing is encouraged but never required. Many participants begin by listening and gradually feel more comfortable participating over time.

What topics are addressed in group therapy?

Topics may include anxiety, depression, stress management, panic disorder, substance use, social skills, anger, and self-esteem.

Are group therapy sessions virtual?

Yes. Our group therapy programs are offered virtually so participants across British Columbia can attend.

How long are the sessions?

Most sessions last approximately 80 minutes. The Supportive Group option is a single 50-minute session.

Who leads the group therapy sessions?

Group therapy sessions are facilitated by Mickey Stein, PhD, who guides discussion and helps participants build practical skills.

How do I register for group therapy?

You can register through our booking page. Spaces are limited and early registration is recommended.
