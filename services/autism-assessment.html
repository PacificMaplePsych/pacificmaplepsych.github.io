While the clinical term is Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), we recognize that many people view autism as a natural variation in how the brain works rather than something ‘disordered.’ Our approach is neurodiversity-affirming and strengths-based.

Autism Assessment Process

Our autism assessments begin with a detailed intake to understand developmental history, current concerns, and goals for the assessment.

We then complete a comprehensive evaluation using interviews, standardized measures, and clinical observation. This may include assessment of social communication, adaptive functioning, behaviour patterns, and cognitive functioning.

Each autism assessment is tailored to the individual, whether you are seeking an adult autism diagnosis in Vancouver or an assessment for a child or teen.

Our team of psychologists in Vancouver specializes in neurodevelopmental assessment and provides a supportive, respectful assessment experience.

We focus on more than diagnosis alone by offering clear explanations, practical recommendations, and guidance on next steps, including school, workplace, or daily life supports.

Many clients come to us for a private autism assessment when waitlists for public services are long or when they are seeking a more individualized evaluation.

Autism Assessment FAQs

How do I get an autism assessment in Vancouver?

You can book a private autism assessment directly with our clinic. We provide assessments in Vancouver without requiring a referral.

Do you offer adult autism diagnosis in Vancouver?

Yes. We provide adult autism assessments and formal diagnostic reports that may be used for workplace accommodations, disability documentation, and personal understanding.

What is the difference between private and public autism assessments in BC?

Public assessments often involve long waitlists. Private autism assessments provide faster access, more flexibility, and a highly individualized evaluation process.

Will I receive a formal autism diagnosis?

Yes. If criteria are met, you will receive a formal diagnosis along with a comprehensive report and recommendations.

Can an autism assessment support school or workplace accommodations?

Yes. Our reports can support accommodations in school, university, and workplace settings, as well as other documentation needs in British Columbia.
